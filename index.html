Regulation Overview COMAR 10.07.05 - All 29 Sections grouped by category
Licensing & Application
.01Purpose - Establish standards for RSAs providing home health services to sick or disabled individuals in their residence
.02Definitions - 29 defined terms including RSA, skilled care, clinical record, governing authority, significant change of condition
.03License Required - No person may operate an RSA without an OHCQ license; no fee to apply; $1,000/3-year renewal
.04Licensing Procedures - Typed application; business plan; org chart; policies per .08B; SDAT good standing; Workers Comp proof; RSA Certification
.05Changes Affecting License - 25%+ stock transfer = new agency; $50 amended license for name/address; 45-day notice for ownership change
.05-1Home Medical Equipment - Special requirements for DME provision (not applicable to non-skilled RSA)
Administration & Governance
.06Public Representation - Accurately represent licensed scope; no false advertising of unlicensed services
.07Inspection by Dept - OHCQ may conduct unannounced inspections; agency must cooperate and provide records
.08Administration - Governing authority; administrator qualifications; written policies required per .08B (5 categories: admin, personnel, patient care, informed consent, environment/safety)
.09Complaint Process - OHCQ Complaint Hotline: 1-800-492-6005; agency must notify clients of hotline number
Personnel & Training
.10Employee Requirements - TB screening before client contact; health screening; skills assessment; criminal background; license/cert verification; OIG LEIE check; worker classification per HG 19-4A-11
.10DCertified Caregivers - CNA required for ADL assistance; CMT required for medication admin under RN delegation
.10EWaiver of Skilled Services - Client may sign waiver if no adverse consequences expected and does not require ADL cert
.11Training - Agency orientation before client contact; outside trainers require OHCQ approval; skills checklist in employee file
Client Services & Care
.12Services Provided - Written service plan; care plan elements; RN supervision frequencies (45d/3mo/4mo); on-call requirements; 6-month plan review
.13Client Representative - Legal authority for decisions; signs informed consent for plan changes or nonlicensed assistance
.14Clinical Records - Security/confidentiality; care notes weekly and upon significant change; record content requirements
.15Maintenance of Records - Client records retained 5+ years post-discharge; privacy/confidentiality; controlled release
.16Client Rights - Informed consent; dignity/privacy; cost estimates; itemized billing; complaint notification; right to refuse
Specialized Services
.17IV/Related Therapies - Special requirements for RSAs providing intravenous or related therapy services (not applicable Year 1)
.18Ventilator Services - Special requirements for RSAs providing ventilator-dependent client care (not applicable Year 1)
.19Home Medical Equipment - DME delivery, installation, maintenance, instruction; Medicare Part B certification path (not applicable Year 1)
Enforcement & Sanctions
.20Sanctions - OHCQ may impose sanctions for regulatory violations; progressive enforcement actions
.21Civil Money Penalties - Financial penalties for regulatory noncompliance; per-violation and per-day assessments
.22CMP Amounts - Penalty amounts determined by violation severity, history, and harm to clients
.23CMP Hearings - Right to administrative hearing to contest civil money penalties
License Actions & Closures
.24Emergency Suspension - Immediate suspension if client health/safety at risk; no prior notice required
.25Denial/Revocation - License denied or revoked for failure to comply; fraud; conviction; inability to operate safely
.26Notice to Clients - Agency must notify clients of closure, suspension, or revocation; ensure continuity of care
.27Unlicensed Operation - Criminal penalty for operating without a license; misdemeanor subject to fine/imprisonment
.28Hearings - Administrative hearing procedures for contested license actions
Required Policies (.08B)
ADMAdministration - Scope of services; admission criteria; client assessment; billing/records; QA program; clinical management
PERPersonnel - Job descriptions; skills assessment; health screening/TB; care supervision requirements; employee file content; discipline
PATPatient Care - Service provision; skilled service criteria; drug admin; enteral/parenteral; monitoring frequency; client/family training
CONInformed Consent - Consent for plan changes; consent for nonlicensed individual assistance with treatments or self-admin of meds
ENVEnvironment/Safety - Formula/IV storage; infection control; biomedical waste; equipment maintenance; emergency procedures
RN Supervision Frequencies COMAR 10.07.05.12E - On-site supervision by Registered Nurse
Medication Administration
45 Days
Staff administering medications under RN delegation. Requires CMT certification. RN on-site supervision at least every 45 days.
Self-Admin Assistance
3 Months
Staff assisting with self-administration of medications (reminders, opening containers). RN on-site supervision every 3 months.
No Medication Role
4 Months
Staff who neither administer nor assist with medications. Companion/homemaker services only. RN on-site supervision every 4 months.

Scope of Services - Non-Skilled RSA (Year 1) COMAR 10.07.05.12 - In-scope and out-of-scope service boundaries per CLIN-001
Personal Care (ADLs)
Bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, oral hygiene, skin care, transfers, mobility assistance. CNA required per .10D when RN determines need.
COMAR 10.07.05.12
Companionship
Conversation, games, reading, accompaniment to appointments, mail assistance, social engagement. Interactive Caregiving applied at every visit.
COMAR 10.07.05.12
Homemaking (IADLs)
Light housekeeping, laundry, meal preparation, dishes, trash removal, organization. No heavy cleaning, major repairs, or financial management.
COMAR 10.07.05.12
Medication Reminders
Verbal prompts only. No administration, syringe prep, IV management. CMT/RN delegation required for actual administration per .10D.
COMAR 10.07.05.10D-E
Respite & Safety Monitoring
Temporary family caregiver relief. Supervision, safety checks, fall prevention, wandering monitoring for cognitively impaired clients.
COMAR 10.07.05.12
Out of Scope (Year 1)
Skilled nursing, wound care, catheter care, injections, IV therapy, PT/OT/ST, medication admin (unless expanded). Refer to home health, hospice, or physician.
